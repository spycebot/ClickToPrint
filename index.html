Shannon Asked: Why is zero factorial equal to one?

The reason zero factorial (0!) is defined to be 1 might seem counterintuitive at first, but it's based on several mathematical conventions and justifications:

Mathematical Conventions

  1. Consistency with combinatorial formulas: In combinatorics, n! represents the number of ways to arrange n distinct objects. When n = 0, there's exactly one way to arrange zero objects (i.e., do nothing). Defining 0! = 1 maintains consistency with these formulas.
  2. Extension of the factorial function: The factorial function can be extended to non-integer values using the gamma function (Γ(n + 1) = n!). Defining 0! = 1 ensures a smooth extension of the factorial function.
  3. Mathematical formulas and identities: Many mathematical formulas, such as the binomial theorem and power series expansions, rely on the convention 0! = 1 to work correctly.

Justification

Defining 0! = 1 might seem arbitrary, but it's a deliberate choice that simplifies many mathematical expressions and formulas. This convention has been widely adopted in mathematics and is now an integral part of the factorial function's definition.

By defining 0! = 1, mathematicians can maintain consistency, simplify formulas, and ensure that various mathematical constructs work seamlessly together.
